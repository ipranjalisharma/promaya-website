Multi-Scale Attribution

Because ProMaya processes data across four different node types (Atom, Residue, Surface, Sequence), the Graph Grad-CAM scores can be aggregated to provide biological insights at multiple resolutions.

1. Residue-Level Binding Maps

By aggregating the scores of all Residue nodes, ProMaya outputs a 1D vector (length \(L\)) representing the binding interface hotspot. - Use Case: Direct validation against known interface residues. Identifies which specific amino acids govern the binding event, guiding alanine scanning mutagenesis experiments.

2. Atom-Level Pharmacophores

By aggregating the scores of the Atom nodes, the attribution map achieves sub-Angstrom resolution. - Use Case: Pinpoints the precise chemical groups driving the interaction (e.g., identifying that a specific aromatic ring on a Tyrosine, rather than the backbone, is providing the \(\pi-\pi\) stacking interaction).

3. Surface Hotspots

By aggregating the scores of the Surface nodes (from the PointNet++ cloud). - Use Case: Visualizes the geometric and electrostatic "patches" on the protein exterior that form the lock-and-key fit, independent of the underlying amino acid sequence.

4. Intrinsic Disorder Region (IDR) Focus

By correlating the Residue attribution scores with the IUPred2A disorder metric. - Use Case: Highlights the role of flexible linker regions in binding. Many proteins undergo "folding upon binding," where a disordered region becomes structured only upon contact with its partner. ProMaya explicitly highlights these transient mechanisms.

[INFO] SHAP Analysis In addition to Grad-CAM, ProMaya supports standard SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) value computation for the XGBoost classification head, allowing users to determine exactly how much the Neural FFN vs. the raw embeddings contributed to the final probability.
