The PPI Challenge¶

Protein-Protein Interactions (PPIs) are the fundamental drivers of cellular function, orchestrating pathways, immune responses, and molecular machineries. Accurate prediction of PPIs from primary sequence and tertiary structure remains a grand challenge in computational biology due to the multi-scale nature of protein interactions—ranging from sub-atomic electron cloud overlapping to global geometric shape complementarity.

The ProMaya Solution¶

ProMaya directly addresses these challenges through a 5-module Hierarchical Multimodal Deep Learning approach. It doesn't just look at amino acids; it parses proteins across four distinct modalities:

  1. Atomic Graph (41-d): Introducing the Local Surface Mass Density (LSMD) hypothesis to capture electron density proxies at binding interfaces.
  2. Residue Graph (1082-d): Fusing biochemical properties, ProtTrans-650M language model embeddings, and intrinsic disorder metrics (IUPred2A).
  3. Surface Point Cloud (14-d): Modeling 3D geometric shape and electrostatic complementarity.
  4. Sequence Context: Leveraging deep evolutionary information from PSSMs.

Unprecedented Interpretability¶

ProMaya isn't a black box. Through a novel application of Graph Grad-CAM and SHAP value analysis, ProMaya provides attribution at multiple scales:

  • Residue-Level Maps: Identifying exact binding interface hotspots.
  • Atom-Level Maps: Highlighting specific chemical groups driving the interaction.
  • IDR Focus: Understanding the role of flexible linker regions in transient binding.
